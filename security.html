Cybersecurity Safety Tips

  • 1. Use Strong Passwords

    Create unique and complex passwords for each account. Avoid using personal information or common words, and use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

  • 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

    Enable 2FA wherever possible to add an extra layer of security to your accounts. This requires a second verification step beyond just a password.

  • 3. Avoid Phishing Scams

    Be cautious with emails and messages from unknown senders. Look for signs of phishing, such as unusual links or attachments, and don't share personal information.

  • 4. Keep Software Updated

    Ensure your operating system, applications, and antivirus software are regularly updated to protect against the latest security threats.

  • 5. Use a VPN on Public Wi-Fi

    When using public Wi-Fi, consider using a VPN to secure your internet connection and protect your data from potential eavesdroppers.

  • 6. Backup Important Data

    Regularly back up important files to an external drive or secure cloud storage in case of data loss due to cyberattacks.
